What is Generative AI?

Generative AI refers to artificial intelligence systems that can create new content -- like text, images,audio,or even code -- based on patterns they've learned from vast amounts of data.

Unlike traditional AI, which is often used for tasks like classification or prediction, generative AI goes a step further by producing original outputs that mimic human creativity.

Generative AI has been making waves across various industries, from entertainment to healthcare, and is reshaping how we interact with technology.

AI can be used to generate realistic images, mimic human speech, or write emails, among other applications.

But with great power comes great responsibility—and risk.

Unfortunately, generative AI can also be used by cybercriminals to craft more convincing and scalable attacks.

Phishing Emails

Traditionally, phishing emails were easy to spot—poor grammar, generic greetings, and suspicious links. But now, generative AI can write highly personalized, grammatically perfect messages that mimic real colleagues or companies.

These emails might:

>
  • Reference your recent projects
  • Use your company's branding
  • Include realistic-looking attachments or links

Deepfake Audio & Video

AI can also generate fake audio or video clips that sound or look like real people. Imagine receiving a voicemail from your manager asking for sensitive data—except it's not really them.

Social Engineering at Scale

Generative AI can automate social engineering attacks, creating fake profiles, chatbots, or even entire websites that trick users into revealing personal or company information.

What You Should Look Out For

So, how can you protect yourself?

  • Verify requests: Always double-check unexpected messages, especially those asking for sensitive info or urgent action.
  • Check the sender: Look closely at email addresses and URLs. Even small differences can signal a fake.
  • Report suspicious activity: If something feels off, trust your instincts and report it to your IT or security team.
  • Stay informed: Cyber threats evolve quickly. Regular training and awareness are your best defense.
